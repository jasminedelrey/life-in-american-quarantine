How Did Our Self's Cope With Isolation?

A survey was sent to the author's friends of friends and families.
Below you will find the respondent's answers to self-reflective questions about their quarantine experience.

During quarantine, I would...

What did people think about their own quarantine experience?

Several students felt that being home felt that it "stunted their growth" and was "emotionally unhealthy" as an independent young adult. Faced with the challenges of being home and learning remotely, students realized the impurities in their school's administration and the spirit of learning to be comfortable with your own company. Many viewed their hope in a post-quarantine world as delirious as they struggled to keep in touch with their friends from school. Evidently, self-quarantine presented many challenges for a college-aged student at a significant time in their lives that they expected to be spending figuring out themselves far from the familiarity of their homes.

Reflecting on their quarantine lives, respondents shared a few lessons they learned...

"Focus on the things you can control in front of you."

"Go outside more!"

"To advocate for myself more and to do more hobbies."

"Friends are everything"

"I realized so much of what I idolize may be achieved by discipline."

"Going through bad experiences and changes can be just as productive and educational as going through good experiences and changes, even though it may suck."
